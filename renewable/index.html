Different energy has different advantages and disadvantages on producing energy. Let's focus on 1 from each side.

Fossil Fuels (Non-Renewable) Solar (Renewable)
What is it?

Fossil Fuels are oil that can be extracted from the underground. They are made out of the remaining of animals, decomposing with sand clay and other minerals.

Solar energy is energy that uses the sun to be powered. Solar energy can easily absorbed by solar panels anywhere the sun shines at. This means that energy could be generated anywhere during daytime.
Advantages

Fossil Fuels are extremely efficient and convenient. Which means that it acts as a perfect fuel to generate energy for day to day use. It is also extremely cheap and capable of generating large amount of energy.

Solar energy only requires one sun (and one universe) and one solar panel to be able to generate energy. It is also capable to mobile energy generating, that means it can generate energy from point to point without having to stop as long as the sun is shining onto it.
Disadvantages

In the process of generating energy using fossil fuels, a large quantity of carbon dioxide will be released, which in the end will not be processed but released to the atmosphere and cause more serious issues on climate change.

Solar only relies on the sun, which means if that day was a rainy/cloudy day, the solar panel will not be able to generate most or any energy. Installing solar panels will also cost a lot and it cannot provide a lot of energy with one panel. 5 panels can provide about one household's' energy.
